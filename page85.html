“Willpower is like a muscle. The more you use it, the stronger it gets.” goes a famous quote.

Willpower is an inner strength that lets you control your emotions, behavior, and attention. It is the ability to do tasks despite inner resistance, laziness, discomfort, difficulty and idleness.

If you develop this skill and overcome procrastination and laziness, focus on what you are doing, and avoid unhealthy or unreasonable temptations.

Willpower gives you firmness, decisiveness, determination, assertiveness, resolution, persistence, and the power of pushing yourself towards your goals and achievements.

WAYS TO DEVELOP WILLPOWER

1 - Evaluate your habits and set a long term for change :

For example, if you cannot eat right and you are gaining weight continuously then, you should avoid vague goals such as “I will eat healthier,” instead be specific with your goals by aligning them to “I will stop drinking caffeinated drinks and eating junk food.” Setting sub-goals such as, “I will not have junk food this week” will make the task even easier.

2 - Sacrifice temporary pleasures :

Temporary pleasures are destructors of willpower. Train your mind to sacrifice the desire of instant pleasures for the long term reward. Always visualize the big picture after taking a small step. This will help in resisting things for the time being.

3 - Meditate :

Meditation can help you to build up your willpower. Use guided meditation if required. There are a lot of guided meditation techniques, which help in concentration and willpower.

Researchers claim that after meditation your brain will be able to focus better, you will have more energy, and you will be less stressed.

4 - Keep track of your involuntary actions :

Involuntary actions determine our will power. So, next time when you find yourself ordering coffee during stress, just stop and ask yourself “Do you really need it?” Keep in mind the long term goal of being healthy and stopping caffeinated drinks.
